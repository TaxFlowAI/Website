**For:** Web designer (marketing/landing site for TaxFlowAI)  
**Product:** TaxFlowAI — client and accountant portals already exist (taxflowai.frontline.financial). This brief is for **one public-facing landing page** (e.g. taxflow.frontline.financial or a dedicated TaxFlowAI URL) that explains the product, drives sign-ups, and supports the brand. Scope: **single page only** — no multi-page site for now.

## 1. What is TaxFlowAI?

TaxFlowAI is a **tax and compliance platform** that connects Australian individuals and businesses with their accountant (E&A Advisory Pty Ltd). It is **powered by / operated by Frontline Financial** (Frontline Holdings Group). The platform offers:

- **For clients:** Upload receipts (AI-scanned and categorised), store documents, track lodgements and deadlines, vehicle logbook for ATO compliance, secure vault, and a friendly AI assistant named **Flo**.
- **For accountants:** CRM, accounts and lodgement pipelines, document vault, engagement letters, ASIC agent services, and workflow tools.

**Tagline in product:** "Your tax, under control."  
**Supporting line:** "Tax made simple. For every Australian."

The **selling points** we want the website to convey (with "extravagant clarity"):

- **Always know what's happening** — No more "where's my return?" or "what do I need to send?" Clients see status, next steps, and deadlines.
- **Stay organised** — Receipts and documents in one place, sorted by Flo; lodgements and tasks visible at a glance.
- **Reach your accountant** — Simple way to upload, message, and get answers without playing phone tag.

## 2. Brand and Visual Direction

### Primary brand

- **Name:** TaxFlowAI (capital F and AI; "TaxFlow" + "AI").
- **Sub-line:** "Powered by Frontline Financial" appears under the logo in the product; the marketing site should make the relationship clear (e.g. "TaxFlowAI is powered by Frontline Financial" or similar) without letting Frontline's own colour scheme override TaxFlowAI's identity where TaxFlowAI is the hero.

### Colours (TaxFlowAI product palette — use for the TaxFlowAI website)

- **Primary accent:** Teal/green `#00FCB8` (vibrant, modern, "go" / "done").
- **Accent hover:** `#00e0a4`.
- **Backgrounds:** Deep navy `#0A1628` (main), darker `#060D1A` (hero/deep areas).
- **Surfaces:** `#0f1729`, `#151d32` for cards/panels.
- **Text:** White `#FFFFFF` for headings and primary copy; muted grey `#94a3b8` for secondary.
- **Borders:** Subtle `rgba(255,255,255,0.08)`.
- **Accent glow:** `rgba(0, 252, 184, 0.25)` for highlights and CTAs.

You may introduce additional tints (e.g. soft blue or purple in gradients) for the marketing site as long as the teal accent and dark base remain the recognisable TaxFlowAI look.

### Typography

- **Display / headings:** Outfit (or similar modern sans).
- **Body:** DM Sans (or similar readable sans).
- Clear hierarchy: bold headlines, readable body, generous spacing so the message ("always know what's happening", "stay organised", "reach your accountant") is unmissable.

### Tone

- **Confident but approachable** — Professional tax tech, not stuffy. Australian audience; warm and direct.
- **Flo** — The in-product AI is "Flo": organised, dryly funny, helpful. The website can hint at Flo (e.g. "Meet Flo", "Flo has your back") without making the whole site about him. Flo supports the idea of "someone's got this" and "no more guessing."

## 3. Target Audiences

- **Primary:** Australian individuals and small-business owners who want a simpler way to do tax with their accountant — upload receipts, see status, stay organised.
- **Secondary:** Accountants and practices (e.g. E&A Advisory) who use or recommend TaxFlowAI for their clients; the site should support "recommend to your clients" or "work with us" if relevant.
- **Trust:** ATO-compliant, secure, encrypted; registered tax agent (E&A Advisory); data in Australia where applicable. The site should feel trustworthy and compliant without sounding like a legal document.

## 4. Single Landing Page — Suggested Sections (all on one page)

Design **one scrolling landing page**. Suggested sections (designer may combine or simplify):

- **Hero:** Strong headline (e.g. "Your tax, under control" or "Always know what's happening with your taxes"). Sub-head that's benefit-led. Primary CTA: Sign up / Get started.
- **Problem / solution (optional block):** Short pain points (lost receipts, unclear status, hard to reach accountant) and how TaxFlowAI fixes them (Flo, vault, status, one place to upload and communicate). Can be folded into hero or one short section.
- **Features (for clients):** Receipts + AI scan, document vault, lodgement visibility, Flo, secure and ATO-aligned. Icons or short visuals; scannable copy. Keep compact so the page doesn't feel long.
- **Trust:** Security, tax agent, "Powered by Frontline Financial," E&A Advisory. One short strip or footer block.
- **Footer:** Links (Privacy, Terms if applicable), contact, TaxFlowAI + Powered by Frontline Financial. **Do not change the existing footer:** keep Privacy Policy, Collection Notice, Terms of Service, Verify Tax Agent. Repeat CTA (Sign up / Get started) if it fits.

Everything above lives on **one page**; no separate "Features" or "About" pages for now.

---

## 5. Design Principles

- **Extravagant clarity** — Headlines and value props should be obvious at a glance. No jargon; lead with benefits.
- **Dark theme** — The product is dark (navy + teal). The marketing site should feel like the same world: dark base, teal accents, clean type. If you need a "light" variant (e.g. for print or accessibility), keep the teal and navy as the core brand colours.
- **Mobile-first** — Many users will be on phone; hero, features, and CTA must work on small screens.
- **Accessibility** — Contrast (white/light grey on dark, teal on dark), focus states, and legible font sizes. WCAG 2.1 AA where possible.
- **Consistency with product** — Visitors who click "Sign up" or "Log in" land in the TaxFlowAI app (dark theme, same teal). The website should feel like a natural prelude to that experience.
